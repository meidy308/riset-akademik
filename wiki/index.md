## Concepts
- [[phd-spine]] - The five essential, interconnected components that form the structural foundation of a coherent and successful PhD project.
- [[research-drift]] - The process by which a research project gradually loses alignment with its original core components, leading to confusion and burnout.
- [[five-minute-health-check]] - A rapid diagnostic tool for researchers to identify misalignment in their PhD spine.
- [[gap-duplication-test]] - A method for validating the strength and uniqueness of a research gap by checking for existing literature that addresses it.
- [[pico-framework]] - A framework used to define the boundaries of a research topic or question, particularly in quantitative research.
- [[nearest-neighbor-paper-test]] - A validation step in topic selection where a researcher identifies the paper most conceptually and methodually similar to their proposed idea to assess potential contribution.
- [[the-narrowing-paradox]] - A phenomenon in research topic development where attempting to find a research gap by narrowing the scope too aggressively leads to a topic that is technically feasible but lacks significance or relevance.
- [[calibration-test]] - A validation technique used to forecast the potential impact and citation count of a proposed research topic by analyzing the performance of similar existing literature.
- [[matthew-effect]] - A sociological phenomenon in academia where initial success leads to further success, creating an intensifying cycle of advantage for certain researchers or institutions.
- [[phd-productivity-framework]] - An approach to managing PhD workloads
- [[batch-execution]] - A practice to grouping similar task together
- [[deep-work]] - Blocking out time to concentrating on a single task
- [[weekly-reset]] - A time block used to audit progress and plan the upcoming week
