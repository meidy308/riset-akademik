## Key Points

- **The Problem:** PhD progress often stalls not due to lack of effort, but due to poor scheduling and lack of structure. Motivation alone is insufficient for the high cognitive load required by research.
- **The 3-Layer System:**
    - **Layer 1: North Star:** A specific, written deliverable to be achieved within a 12-week period (e.g., "write literature review chapter").
    - **Layer 2: Weekly Commitments:** 3–5 realistic and precise goals for the upcoming week (e.g., "write 500 words of methodology" instead of "work on methodology").
    - **Layer 3: Daily Next Actions:** Bite-sized, 5-10 minute tasks to ease into the workday and reduce procrastination.
- **Weekly Reset (20 Minutes):** A crucial ritual to maintain momentum.
    - **Inventory:** List all current work/projects.
    - **Reality Check:** Count actual deep work blocks completed vs. planned.
    - **Pick Commitments:** Select 3–5 goals for the next week.
    - **Schedule Focus Blocks:** Block out time for deep work first, then build around it.
    - **Define Next Actions:** Break down each commitment into immediate start steps.
- **Scheduling Strategy:** Use "Lab/Computational Days" (intense, no distractions) and "Desk Days" (writing, reading, admin). Group admin tasks (emails, etc.) into small, specific clusters to prevent fragmentation of focus.
