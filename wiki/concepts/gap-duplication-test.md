## Definition
A simple validation technique used to ensure that a proposed research gap is "real" and not already addressed by existing literature.

## How It Works
The researcher actively searches for papers or studies that are very close to their proposed topic. If multiple papers already address the exact same phenomenon, the "gap" is considered closed (or "dead on arrival"), meaning the project lacks novelty.

## Key Parameters
- **Strength of Gap**: The degree of uniqueness and significance of the identified void in knowledge.
- **Duplication Level**: How many existing studies have already addressed or partially addressed the proposed topic.

## When To Use
Crucial during the initial phase of gap identification and as part of the "5-Minute Health Check" to prevent working on unpublishable topics.

## Risks & Pitfalls
- **False Negatives**: Assuming a gap exists because you haven't searched thoroughly enough.
- **Over-narrowing**: Trying so hard to find a unique gap that the topic becomes too niche or lacks significance.
