## Definition
The practice of grouping similar tasks together to be completed in a single, dedicated time block.

## How It Works
Instead of responding to interruptions (like emails or admin requests) as they arrive, you "cluster" them into specific windows of time. In the context of PhD productivity, this applies to:
- **Admin Clusters:** Grouping all emails, form filling, and small logistics in one period.
- **Lab/Computational Blocks:** Dedicating entire days to intensive, hands-on research activities without switching to desk-based writing.

## Key Parameters
- **Granularity:** The size of the cluster (e.g., a 1-hour "admin box").
- **Frequency:** How often clusters are scheduled (e.g., daily vs. weekly).

## When To Use
- To prevent "context switching" and fragmentation of focus during deep work sessions.
- To manage low-cognitive-load tasks that would otherwise interrupt high-cognitive-load research.

## Risks & Pitfalls
- **Underestimating Volume:** Failing to allocate enough time, causing admin tasks to bleed into deep work blocks.
- **Over-batching:** Creating clusters so large that they become overwhelming or lose the sense of urgency.
