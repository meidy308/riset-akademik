## Definition
A phenomenon in research topic development where attempting to find a research gap by narrowing the scope too aggressively leads to a topic that is technically feasible but lacks significance or relevance.

## How It Works
When researchers sense their topic is too broad, they often "shrink to safety" by narrowing the population (e.g., focusing on a single small region) or outcome. While this makes finding a gap easier, it can move the research away from the main academic debate.

## Key Parameters
- **Scope**: The breadth of the population/outcome being studied.
- **Relevance**: The importance of the findings to the wider field.

## When To Use
Understanding this paradox is crucial when using frameworks like PICO to prevent creating "peripheral" research that no one in the field cares about.

## Risks & Pitfalls
- **Irrelevance**: Creating a study that is so specific (e.g., a case study of an obscure region) that it lacks appeal for high-impact, generalist journals.
