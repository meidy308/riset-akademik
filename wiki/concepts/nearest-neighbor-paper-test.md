## Definition
A validation step in topic selection where a researcher identifies the paper most conceptually and methodologically similar to their proposed idea to assess potential contribution.

## How It Works
1. **Identify**: Use tools like Google Scholar to find the "nearest neighbor"—the paper closest to yours in terms of topic, method, or population.
2. **Challenge**: Ask a "brutal" question: "What does my paper do that goes over and above this one?" 
3. **Evaluate**: Determine if your work is a significant advancement or merely a duplication/minor tweak (e.g., just changing the population).

## Key Parameters
- Conceptual similarity
- Methodological similarity
- Population overlap

## When To Use
During the early stages of topic development to prevent investing months or years into a "dead end" topic that lacks a clear research gap.

## Risks & Pitfalls
- **Duplication**: Mistaking a minor tweak (like changing the country/population) for a significant contribution if the core mechanism is already well-established.
