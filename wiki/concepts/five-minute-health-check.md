## Definition
A quick, structured evaluation process used to assess the alignment and coherence of a research project's fundamental components (the PhD spine).

## How It Works
The check involves asking five targeted questions, one for each stage of the research spine:

1.  **Gap Validation**: Is my research gap strong, unique, and does it pass a "duplication test"?
2.  **Question Alignment**: Does my research question directly address and speak to this specific gap?

3.  **Interest/Claim Alignment**: Does answering this research question lead me toward the core claim or idea I am actually interested in demonstrating?
4.  **Scope Check**: Is my thesis aim properly scoped, integrating the gap, the question, and the intended claim into a deliverable package?
5.  **Methodological Feasibility**: Do my chosen methods provide a realistic and effective way to demonstrate/test my research question and claims?

## When To Use
- At the beginning of a new research project.
- When progress feels unusually difficult or "heavy."
- Following significant feedback from supervisors or peers.
- Periodically throughout the PhD to prevent gradual drift.
