## Definition
A short (approx. 20-minute) periodic ritual used to audit recent progress and plan the upcoming week, ensuring alignment with long-term goals.

## How It Works
The process involves five key steps:
1. **Inventory:** List everything currently on your plate (experiments, admin, side projects).
2. **Reality Check:** Quantify how many deep work blocks you *actually* completed vs. what was planned.
3. **Pick Commitments:** Select 3–5 realistic and precise goals for the next week.
4. **Schedule Focus Blocks First:** Block out time for high-priority, cognitively demanding tasks before anything else.
5. **Define Next Actions:** Break down each commitment into tiny, "bite-sized" starting steps (e.s., 5–10 minutes) to lower the barrier to entry.

## Key Parameters
- **Duration:** ~20 minutes.
- **Frequency:** Weekly.
- **Primary Goal:** Maintaining momentum and preventing research drift.

## When To Use
- Every week, typically at the end of a work week or start of a new one.
- Whenever you feel "lost" in your PhD chaos or sense you are drifting from your thesis goals.

## Risks & Pitfalls
- **Lack of Honesty:** Skipping the "reality check" phase and over-planning based on optimistic assumptions rather than actual capacity.
- **Vague Planning:** Failing to define specific "next actions," making it harder to start work the following week.
